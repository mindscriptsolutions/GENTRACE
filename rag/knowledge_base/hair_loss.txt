Androgenetic alopecia is the most common form of hereditary hair loss, affecting both men and women. DHT (dihydrotestosterone) is the primary hormonal driver of androgenetic alopecia, causing miniaturization of hair follicles. FDA-approved treatments include minoxidil (topical) and finasteride (oral for men), which slow progression and promote regrowth. Early intervention at Norwood stages 2-3 yields the best treatment outcomes. Minoxidil 5% applied twice daily is effective for both male and female pattern hair loss. Finasteride 1mg daily reduces DHT levels by approximately 70% and stabilizes hair loss in most men. Low-level laser therapy (LLLT) is an emerging non-pharmacological treatment with evidence for mild to moderate androgenetic alopecia. Nutritional deficiencies in iron, zinc, biotin, and vitamin D can accelerate hereditary hair loss and should be corrected. Stress management is important as chronic stress can trigger telogen effluvium, worsening androgenetic alopecia. Platelet-rich plasma (PRP) therapy shows promising results for hair follicle stimulation. Hair transplant surgery is an effective option for advanced Norwood stages 5-7. Avoiding tight hairstyles and heat damage preserves existing hair in genetically susceptible individuals. Regular scalp massage may improve blood circulation to hair follicles. Consulting a dermatologist early maximizes treatment effectiveness for hereditary hair loss.
