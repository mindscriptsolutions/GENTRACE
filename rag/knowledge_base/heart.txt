Cardiovascular disease prevention requires a multi-factorial approach targeting modifiable risk factors. Smoking cessation is the single most impactful intervention, reducing cardiac event risk by 50% within one year of quitting. Regular aerobic exercise of at least 150 minutes per week strengthens the heart muscle and improves cholesterol profiles. A heart-healthy diet low in saturated fats, trans fats, and cholesterol reduces LDL levels and arterial plaque formation. Controlling LDL cholesterol below 100 mg/dL is recommended for high-risk individuals according to ACC/AHA guidelines. Managing hypertension through lifestyle and medication reduces stroke and heart attack risk significantly. Diabetes control is critical as elevated blood glucose damages coronary arteries over time. Limiting alcohol intake reduces the risk of cardiomyopathy and arrhythmias. Stress management reduces cortisol-driven inflammation that contributes to atherosclerosis. Omega-3 fatty acids from fish, flaxseed, and walnuts have cardioprotective effects. Annual lipid panel screening is recommended for individuals with a family history of heart disease. Aspirin therapy may be considered for high-risk individuals under physician guidance. Maintaining a healthy BMI reduces cardiac workload and inflammatory markers. Early-onset family history of heart disease before age 55 in fathers or 65 in mothers significantly elevates hereditary risk.
