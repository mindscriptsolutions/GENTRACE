Type 2 diabetes prevention is strongly supported by lifestyle modification. The CDC Diabetes Prevention Program shows that losing 5-7% of body weight reduces diabetes risk by 58% in high-risk individuals. Regular physical exercise of at least 150 minutes per week of moderate-intensity activity significantly improves insulin sensitivity. A diet low in refined carbohydrates and high in fiber helps regulate blood glucose levels. Monitoring fasting blood glucose annually is recommended for individuals with a family history of diabetes. Reducing sugar-sweetened beverages and processed foods lowers glycemic load. The WHO recommends limiting free sugars to less than 10% of total energy intake. Metformin may be considered for very high-risk individuals under physician guidance. Early detection through HbA1c screening every 1-2 years is advised for those with hereditary risk. Smoking cessation reduces insulin resistance and lowers diabetes risk. Stress management through mindfulness and adequate sleep supports metabolic health. A Mediterranean-style diet rich in vegetables, legumes, whole grains, and healthy fats is associated with reduced diabetes incidence. Regular blood pressure and cholesterol monitoring is important as these conditions often co-occur with diabetes.
