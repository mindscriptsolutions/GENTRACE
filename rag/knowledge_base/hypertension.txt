Hypertension prevention and management relies heavily on lifestyle modification. Reducing sodium intake to less than 2300 mg per day significantly lowers blood pressure according to the American Heart Association. The DASH diet, rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and low-fat dairy, reduces systolic blood pressure by 8-14 mmHg. Regular aerobic exercise of 30 minutes most days of the week lowers blood pressure by 4-9 mmHg. Limiting alcohol consumption to no more than one drink per day for women and two for men reduces hypertension risk. Stress reduction techniques including meditation, yoga, and deep breathing exercises help manage blood pressure. Maintaining a healthy body weight is one of the most effective lifestyle interventions for blood pressure control. Smoking cessation is critical as nicotine raises blood pressure and damages blood vessel walls. Annual blood pressure screening is recommended for all adults, especially those with a family history of hypertension. Potassium-rich foods such as bananas, sweet potatoes, and spinach help counteract the effects of sodium on blood pressure. Caffeine intake should be moderated in hypertension-prone individuals. Sleep apnea treatment can significantly improve blood pressure control. Regular monitoring at home with a validated device helps track blood pressure trends between clinical visits.
