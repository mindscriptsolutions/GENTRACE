About GeneTrace

A research-grade AI system for hereditary disease risk prediction using pedigree analysis and ensemble machine learning.

Overview

Project Overview

GeneTrace is an IEEE-quality final-year research project that predicts hereditary disease risk by analyzing multi-generational family medical history. The system combines paternal and maternal inheritance patterns with personal lifestyle factors to generate risk estimates for Type 2 Diabetes, Hypertension, Cardiovascular Disease, and Androgenetic Alopecia.

🧬 Research Novelty

The Phenotype-Weighted Inheritance Scoring Mechanism (PWIS) is the core contribution. It quantifies hereditary burden by combining lineage weight, disease severity, and age of onset into a single interpretable score.

🤖 Ensemble Learning

Three models — Random Forest, XGBoost, and MLP — are combined using soft-voting ensemble strategy. Each model contributes its probability estimate, and the final prediction is the weighted average.

🔍 Explainable AI

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) provides feature-level explanations for every prediction, identifying which family history factors most strongly influence the hereditary risk estimate.

💊 RAG Recommendations

A Retrieval-Augmented Generation pipeline retrieves relevant passages from WHO, NIH, and CDC knowledge bases to generate personalized preventive recommendations.

Core Formula

PWIS Formula

The mathematical foundation of GeneTrace's hereditary scoring mechanism

PWIS = Σ [ lineage_weight × (severity_weight + onset_weight) ]

Lineage Weights:
  Parent (1st degree)      = 1.0
  Grandparent (2nd degree) = 0.5

Severity Weights:
  None     = 0.00  |  Mild     = 0.25
  Moderate = 0.60  |  Severe   = 1.00

Onset Weights:
  Early onset  (< 45 years)  = 1.0
  Mid onset    (45–59 years) = 0.6
  Late onset   (≥ 60 years)  = 0.3
  No disease   (onset = 0)   = 0.0
Architecture

How the System Works

GeneTrace combines pedigree-based feature engineering with ensemble machine learning to deliver accurate, explainable hereditary risk predictions.

  • ✓
    Family history collected across 2 generations (parents + grandparents)
  • ✓
    PWIS scores computed for diabetes and heart disease
  • ✓
    Interaction features engineered for each disease model
  • ✓
    Ensemble of RF + XGBoost + MLP with soft voting
  • ✓
    SHAP TreeExplainer on XGBoost base estimator
  • ✓
    RAG retrieval from WHO/NIH/CDC knowledge base

⚠ Important Disclaimer

GeneTrace provides hereditary risk estimates only. It is not a medical diagnosis system and does not use DNA sequencing or genomic analysis. All predictions are probabilistic estimates based on family history patterns. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional for medical advice.
